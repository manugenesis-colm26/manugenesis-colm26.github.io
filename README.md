## Overview

The **ManuGenesis** workshop focuses on how agentic AI systems can be used in real design, manufacturing, and supply chain workflows. Large language models can now plan multi-step actions, use tools, and work with humans in the loop. We want to bring this to settings where decisions are tightly connected: design depends on engineering constraints, manufacturing depends on available tools and processes, and supply chains have to deal with uncertainty, cost, and downstream needs.

We invite submissions on topics including but not limited to:

- **LLM Agents for Design & Engineering** -- Language models and agents for design, engineering analysis, process planning, scheduling, and supply-chain reasoning
- **Tool-Using & Multimodal Systems** -- Systems that interface with simulators, digital twins, robotics, and industrial data platforms
- **Reliability, Safety & Oversight** -- Interpretability, safety, and human-AI collaboration in manufacturing environments
- **Benchmarks & Evaluation** -- Datasets, evaluation protocols, and case studies for manufacturing-centered agentic AI
- **Process Planning & Scheduling** -- AI-driven production planning, resource allocation, and supply chain coordination
- **Supply Chain Intelligence** -- Agentic systems for supply chain decision-making under real constraints
